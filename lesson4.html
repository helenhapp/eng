Grammar: Future Simple

Future Simple Tense

We use Future Simple to talk about things that will happen in the future.

Приклади використання:

Ситуація Приклад
Офіційне оголошення
  • The court will open tomorrow at 9 AM.
  • The detective will speak to the press next week.
  • The winter holidays will begin soon.
Спонтанне рішення
  • "The suspect is running away!" — "I'll catch him!"
  • "It's dark in the library." — "I'll turn on the light."
  • "We don't have any clues." — "I'll search the garden."
Передбачення
  • I think the suspect will be nervous during the interview.
  • I am confident we will find the evidence.
  • The thief will eventually make a mistake.
Обіцянка
  • Don't be worried. I'll keep your secret.
  • I promise I won't reveal his motive.
  • We will catch the guilty person, I swear!
Пропозиція / готовність
  • You look upset. I'll make you a cup of tea.
  • That box with evidence is heavy. I'll carry it for you.
  • I'll check the alibi if you want.
Можливість (з maybe, perhaps)
  • Maybe the detective will find a new clue tomorrow.
  • Perhaps she will feel relieved after the truth is revealed.
  • Maybe the innocent man will go home soon.

Формула:

Тип Структура Приклад
+ subject + will + base verb She will find the evidence.
– subject + will not + base verb He will not reveal his alibi.
? Will + subject + base verb? Will she be nervous?

Скорочення:

Повна форма Скорочення
I / you / we / they will I'll / you'll / we'll / they'll
he / she / it will he'll / she'll / it'll
will not won't (НЕ "want"!)

⚠️ Скорочення не вживаються у формальному контексті (ділові листи, офіційні оголошення).

Короткі відповіді:

  • Will she solve the case? → Yes, she will. / No, she won't.
  • ⚠️ У позитивній відповіді скорочення неможливе: Yes, she'll.

Правило умовних речень (If / When):

Після слів if / when / as soon as / before / after ми використовуємо тільки Present Simple, ніколи не ставимо will:

  • ❌ If he will find the clue...
  • ✅ If he finds the clue...
  • ❌ When she will arrive...
  • ✅ When she arrives...

Case: Lady Weston's Diamond Ring

You are the Detective!

Read the story, look at the clues, and solve the mystery.

The Story

Last night, between 19:00 and 21:00, Lady Weston had a party at her big house. During the party, her expensive diamond ring disappeared!

The ring was in a locked jewelry box in her bedroom. The key was hidden on a small hook near the door. It was not in an obvious place. Only someone who knows the house very well could find it quickly.

The box was opened carefully with the key. The lock is not broken.

The Suspects

1. James (The Butler)

  • Behaves calmly and confidently.
  • He says he was serving food in the dining room the whole evening.
  • The cook confirms his alibi: James never left the dining room between 19:00 and 21:00.
  • He is a very good artist and likes to draw.
  • He has bright green eyes and red hair.

2. Anna (The Maid)

  • Looks nervous and worried.
  • She went upstairs for 20 minutes (from 19:30 to 19:50). She said she needed extra napkins.
  • She cleans every room, so she knows the house very well.
  • She asked Lady Weston for extra money last week, but Lady Weston said no.

3. Mr. Fox (The Guest)

  • Looks very surprised when you talk to him.
  • Left the party for 5 minutes (from 20:15 to 20:20) to "get a jacket from his room".
  • He has big debts and desperately needs money.
  • This is his first time in the house. He doesn't know where things are.
  • He also has bright green eyes and red hair.

The Clues

  • The thief opened the box with the key. The thief knew exactly where to look.
  • 5 minutes is not enough time for a stranger to find the key and the ring... unless someone helped him.
  • You searched the fireplace and found a piece of paper that looks like a fragment of a hand-drawn map of the house.
  • Mr. Fox and James look very similar. It is a rare combination of eye and hair color.

Exercise 5: Analyze the Clues

Before you arrest anyone, think about the clues! Answer these questions aloud using full sentences and the word will.

  1. Will Anna become one of the suspects? Why is 20 minutes important?
  2. What will James say about that map that you found?
  3. Will Mr. Fox feel nervous when you show him the map? Why?
  4. Will you ask James and Mr. Fox if they are family? If yes, how will you explain your reasons for asking such a question?

Exercise 6: Solve the Case

Now, finish the investigation! Answer the questions below using the Future Simple tense.

  1. Who will you say is definitely innocent?
  2. Who will you accuse of stealing the ring?
  3. What was their motive? What would they do with the ring if they weren't caught?
  4. Will the guilty suspects confess when you reveal the truth? Or will they somehow be able to avoid responsibility?

Sentence starters:

  • I will arrest... because ...
  • I will say that ... is...
  • Their motive was... They will probably...
  • I am confident they will (not) confess because...
  • The evidence will (not) be enough to prove them guilty.
Reveal the Truth (Solution)

Who did it?
James (the Butler) and Mr. Fox worked together! They are actually brothers (that is why they both have red hair and bright green eyes).

How did they do it?
James stayed in the dining room all evening to have a perfect alibi. But, because he is a good artist, he drew a detailed map of the house for his brother. Mr. Fox used the map to go straight to the bedroom, find the hidden key, and steal the ring in just 5 minutes.

What was their motive?
Mr. Fox has large debts. They planned to sell the ring and share the money!
(Anna the Maid is completely innocent!)
